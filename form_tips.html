Travel Tips

1. There is no need to tip

if you tip, the waiter may think you forgot your change and chase you away. For example, if you participate in a guided tour or cooking class, one way to show your appreciation is to bring a small gift from your home country.

2. Be polite

Foreigners are known to be noisy, especially on public transportation. It is rude to answer your cell phone, and if you are listening to music, please turn down the volume. Blowing your nose in public is more rude than sniffing, and avoid eating on the go.

3. Buy a Suica or Pasmo card

The prepaid card, similar to the Oyster card in London, can be used on most subways and buses and can be recharged in the station. A small deposit must be paid, most of which will be returned if the card is surrendered at the end of the trip. The card can be cheaper than a paper ticket, especially on trips with transfers. Also, with a card, you don't have to stop and wait in line to print a ticket every time you get on a train.
