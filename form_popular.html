Plan your trip

1. Onsen

There are many countries in the world with good hot springs, but when it comes to natural hot springs, Japan is number one. Because of its location in the Pacific Rim of Volcanoes, all prefectures have natural hot springs. The mineral-rich hot springs are effective in relieving a variety of physical ailments such as joint pain, muscle pain, and rough skin. In addition, staying at a ryokan and soaking in a hot spring bath can be very relaxing and refreshing. Enjoying winter sports such as skiing and snowboarding in a hot spring resort is also recommended. After a day on the slopes, warming up in an open-air bath while breathing in the cool air is a special treat. Here is a video about the Onsen:

2. Ryokan

As you travel around Japan, you will have the opportunity to stay in many different types of hotels. Business hotels are cost-effective and ideal for stays in large cities such as Tokyo and Osaka, but you may also want to spend a night at a ryokan. A stay at a traditional Japanese ryokan is a great way to experience the best of Japanese hospitality all at once. Not only will you be treated well, but you will also enjoy sumptuous meals prepared with seasonal ingredients and a hot spring bath next to the baths. many people do not leave the ryokan very often, if only for one night, but staying at a luxury ryokan is a very relaxing experience. Here is a video about the Ryokan:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FdYiNdI2kPE

3. Sushi Making

Of course, eating at a top-notch sushi restaurant during your stay in Japan is a must, but making your own sushi in a sushi class is another great way to enjoy Japan's most famous food. Sushi classes in Japan are often taught by professional sushi chefs, who can teach you about the history, traditions, and cultural significance of the delicacy, as well as tips on how to make sushi at home. If you are so inclined, you should also buy a high-end Japanese knife to make it easier to make delicious sushi. If you don't know what to buy, why not book a half-day private guide in Tokyo to give you advice and guide you through the shopping process, eliminating the language barrier.
